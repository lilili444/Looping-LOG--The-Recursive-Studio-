Methodology

This project adopts The McDonaldization of society(Ritzer, 2000) as its primary methodology to explore the causes that lead individuals into dissociative states. The relentless pursuit of efficiency has created mechanized systems from which no one can escape, mirroring the absolute correctness and conformity of collectivism found in dystopian narratives(Замятин Евгений, 2018). When individuals face the high pressure brought by such efficiency-driven systems, some respond by freezing, as described in the 4F reaction model(Walker, 2013), with a portion of them entering dissociative states.

Dissociation manifests as a loss of time perception and memory. To conceptualize how memory can be expressed in the artwork, media archaeology provides insight into the relationship between websites and memory(Jussi Parikka & Polity Press, 2012). Within this experience of memory loss, each day becomes an endless repetition, and as time flows, the self is ultimately erased. Difference and Repetition(Deleuze, 1968/2014) offers further exploration into these two concepts, deepening the philosophical foundation of the project.
